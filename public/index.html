Understanding Musical Intervals

An interval is the distance between two musical notes. Understanding intervals is fundamental to music theory as they form the building blocks of melodies, harmonies, and chords.

Basic Concepts

Intervals are measured in two ways:

  • Semitones (half steps): The smallest distance between two notes
  • Tones (whole steps): Equal to two semitones

Types of Intervals

Intervals are classified as:

  • Perfect: Unison, Fourth, Fifth, Octave
  • Major/Minor: Seconds, Thirds, Sixths, Sevenths
  • Augmented/Diminished: Modified versions of perfect or major intervals

Quality and Character

Different intervals create distinct emotional qualities:

  • Perfect intervals sound stable and consonant
  • Major intervals tend to sound bright and happy
  • Minor intervals often sound darker or melancholic
  • Augmented/Diminished intervals create tension

Common Uses

Intervals are essential for:

  • Building chords and harmonies
  • Creating melodies
  • Understanding scales
  • Ear training and sight-singing

Pro Tip: Practice recognizing intervals by associating them with familiar song beginnings. For example, "Somewhere Over the Rainbow" starts with an octave jump!
